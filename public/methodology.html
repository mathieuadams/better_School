Our Methodology

How FindSchool.uk builds every overall_rating and keeps the numbers transparent

What goes into a school’s score (overall_rating)

We combine four components on a 0–10 scale to create each school’s overall_rating. The target weights are academic performance against national benchmarks (35%), academic performance versus the local authority (35%), attendance (10%), and Ofsted (20%). When any component is missing the weight is removed and the remaining factors are re-scaled so the final score still sits on a 0–10 range.

Formula
overall_rating = weighted_average(
    national_component,
    la_component,
    attendance_score,
    ofsted_score
  with weights present among those available
)
A) Academic vs National
35% weight

For each subject we hold (English, Maths, Science) we compare the school’s score to the national average for the same school phase (Primary, Secondary, Sixth Form, Special). We linearly scale the difference within a ±20% band: ≥ +20% above national becomes 10, ≤ −20% below becomes 0, with proportional scores in between. We average the available subjects and ignore any missing entries before feeding the 0–10 result into the weighted average.

B) Academic vs Local Authority
35% weight

We repeat the same subject-by-subject scaling using the local authority average as the anchor. Each subject is compared to the LA benchmark for the matching school type, translated into the ±20% band, and averaged to produce another 0–10 input.

C) Attendance
10% weight

We convert the latest overall absence figure into attendance (100 − absence) and scale it linearly so 80% attendance maps to 1 and 97% or higher maps to 10. Scores outside the range are clamped. This keeps attendance aligned with how the government highlights persistent absence.

D) Ofsted
20% weight

Latest Ofsted overall effectiveness is mapped directly to a 0–10 contribution: Outstanding → 10, Good → 8, Requires Improvement → 5, Inadequate → 2. If no inspection exists we drop this component and re-normalise the remaining ones.

Missing data handling

Any component without valid inputs is excluded, the remaining weights are re-scaled, and the published overall_rating clearly notes which slices were present. We also calculate rating_percentile using a percent-rank over overall_rating so parents can see how a school compares to others nationally.

Rating Scale Interpretation

The overall_rating is always expressed on a 0–10 scale, where higher numbers indicate stronger performance across the available components. The ranges below show how to interpret the final results.

9-10
Outstanding
Consistently exceptional outcomes relative to peers and benchmarks.
7-8
Good
Above-average performance with strong inspected and academic indicators.
5-6
Average
Meeting expected standards overall with a balance of strengths and gaps.
3-4
Below Average
Performance trails local or national comparators across multiple measures.
1-2
Poor
Significant concerns with persistent underperformance and/or inspections.

Where the numbers come from

Every figure is pulled from official UK government releases. We align table columns directly with the inputs used in the methodology so users can verify the source data.

  • School test scores: uk_schools.english_score, math_score, science_score
  • National averages: uk_schools.english_avg_national, math_avg_national, science_avg_national
  • Local authority averages: uk_schools.english_avg_la, math_avg_la, science_avg_la (or calculated on the fly per LA/type when not prefilled)
  • Attendance: Latest overall_absence_rate from uk_absence_data
  • Ofsted: Most recent overall effectiveness from uk_ofsted_inspections

When overall_rating is already persisted on uk_schools the API serves that value. If it is missing the route recomputes the score using the same rules outlined above before responding.

How each page uses the score

Different parts of FindSchool.uk surface the score slightly differently, but they all rely on the same rules above.

  • School detail page: Builds a rating box from the four components, hiding any missing slice and re-weighting what remains.
  • Local Authority – Top Rated: Fetches all schools in the selected LA for the active school_type_group tab (Primary, Secondary, Sixth Form, Special) and orders by overall_rating descending, falling back to an Ofsted-only ranking if academic scores are unavailable.
  • City – Top Rated: Mirrors the LA view and orders schools within each tab by overall_rating, now respecting the city flags correctly.
  • Northern Ireland: We do not publish overall ratings; only attendance and contextual details are shown until comparable datasets are released.

Transparency & limitations

We publish the full methodology so families know exactly how each rating is built. Data can lag behind real-world change, so we encourage parents to view ratings as a starting point, visit schools, and combine the numbers with their own judgement.
